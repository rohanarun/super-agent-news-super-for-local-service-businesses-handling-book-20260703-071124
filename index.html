How Super fits into the agent landscape

ChatGPT

World‑class conversational AI for writing and reasoning. Increasingly agentic, but best for one‑off tasks rather than durable operational workflows.

Gemini

Google is pushing computer use with Gemini Spark on desktop, highlighting how valuable real app control has become.

Siri

Voice‑first assistant embedded in Apple devices. Great for quick commands, not full booking or quoting pipelines.

Grok

Opinionated assistant with real‑time context. Useful for insight, less focused on operating business software.

Folk

Niche tools inside the broader automation market. Often require manual setup and don’t reuse execution state.

Orchids

Experimental approaches to agents and automation, still early for mission‑critical service workflows.

Super

Designed for local service operations that repeat every day. Real computer use, plus a reusable computer-use cache for bookings, quotes, and replies.

Why this matters now

Service businesses are rapidly adopting AI agents that can qualify leads and book appointments automatically, as seen with products like Superhuman Agent and Superbench AI. At the same time, major platforms like Google are rolling out computer‑using agents such as Gemini Spark, validating the shift toward agents that act — not just chat.
